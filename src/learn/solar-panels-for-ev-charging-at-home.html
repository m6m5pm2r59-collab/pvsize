How Many Solar Panels for EV Charging at Home?

EV charging can add a meaningful load to your home. A planning estimate often starts with miles driven per day, the EV's kWh per mile, and whether you want solar to offset only the car or the whole home.

Short answer: many EV drivers need a few extra kW of solar capacity for charging, but the exact number depends on driving habits and charging timing.

Inputs to Estimate EV Solar Needs

  • Daily miles: more miles means more kWh to replace.
  • Vehicle efficiency: many EVs use roughly 0.25-0.40 kWh per mile, but check your model.
  • Charging schedule: daytime charging uses solar directly; nighttime charging may rely on grid credits or a battery.
  • Whole-home target: add EV usage to your home kWh before sizing the full system.
