5 kW vs 10 kW solar system

Direct answer: a 10 kW system has roughly twice the DC capacity and annual output of a 5 kW system, but it also needs roughly twice the panel surface and a home load or export arrangement that can use the energy. With 400W panels, plan on about 13 panels for a 5 kW class and 25 panels for 10 kW.

Generation formula

annual kWh = system kW x peak sun hours/day x 365 x 0.80

At 4.5 peak sun hours, 5 x 4.5 x 365 x 0.80 = 6,570 kWh/year. Doubling capacity to 10 kW gives 13,140 kWh/year under the same assumptions. Real output is not perfectly linear when roof directions, inverter clipping, shade, export limits, or curtailment differ between designs.

Which size is more appropriate?

5 kW is the stronger starting point whenannual use is near 5,000-8,000 kWh, roof area is limited, or local export compensation makes oversizing unattractive.
10 kW is the stronger starting point whenannual use is near 10,000-16,000 kWh, loads include an EV or electric heating, and roof plus utility rules support the capacity.
Neither number is enough alonewithout monthly electricity use, peak sun hours, roof fit, tariff/export rules, shading, and future-load assumptions.

Country and regional parameters to check

  • United States: utility interconnection limits, net metering/export rates, roof setbacks, incentives, and actual annual kWh.
  • Australia: distributor export limits, inverter capacity, phase configuration, high-sun yield, and daytime self-consumption.
  • United Kingdom: lower annual sun, roof orientation, export tariff, DNO requirements, and winter-heavy electricity loads.
Error boundary: installed capacity does not by itself determine savings. Electricity rates, self-consumption, export compensation, system price, incentives, degradation, maintenance, and financing change the financial result.
