Formula and assumptions

required kW = monthly kWh x 12 / (365 x peak sun hours x 0.80)

The panel count is required kW x 1,000 / panel watts, always rounded up to a whole panel. Installed DC capacity is the rounded panel count multiplied by panel wattage. All tables use 20% aggregate system losses, equal to an 80% planning efficiency.

  • Module footprints: 19.4 sq ft for 350W, 18.7 sq ft for 400W, and 21.2 sq ft for 450W.
  • No battery, export limit, demand charge, incentive, snow, shade, or seasonal mismatch is modeled.
  • Peak sun hours are annual planning averages, not daylight duration.
  • Final roof design must account for fire setbacks, walkways, vents, orientation, structure, local code, and inverter limits.

Worked example: 1,000 kWh/month, 4.5 sun hours, 400W panels

Required capacity = 12,000 / (365 x 4.5 x 0.80) = 9.13 kW. Dividing by 0.40 kW per panel gives 22.83, so the planning count rounds up to 23 panels. Installed DC capacity is 9.20 kW and module footprint is about 431 sq ft.
